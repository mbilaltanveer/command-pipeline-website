# C17.ai — Website Reference Study
_Source: https://www.c17.ai/_

---

## Identity & Positioning

- **Name:** C17 Lab
- **Category:** "Revenue Engineering Firm" — deliberately not an "agency"
- **Primary offer:** No-cost pilot campaign — radical risk reversal as the main CTA
- **Tagline vibe:** "We engineer systems that drive revenue"
- **Target:** B2B companies with unpredictable pipeline, no dedicated SDR team, or wasted ad spend

| Role | Value |

| Primary text | `#00092C` — deep near-black navy |

| Accent light | `#8A5FFF` — lighter purple |
| Purple tints | `#EDE3FC`, `#EBE1FF` — card/section fills |
| Dark pullquote bg | `#1C2517` — forest dark (used for one testimonial section only) |
| Card background | `#FFFFFF` |
| Body text | `#525562`, `#3F3F3F` |

**Strategy:** Electric purple used sparingly — only on CTAs, brand marks, and progress indicators. Everything else is neutral. Maximum contrast on minimum surface area.

---

## Typography

| Use | Font |
|-----|------|
| Display / hero headlines | `Pp Editorial New` — editorial serif |
| Body / UI | `Inter` |
| System fallback | `SF Pro Display`, `sans-serif` |

**Key move:** The hero H1 mixes roman + *italic* within the same sentence. "We engineer systems" is upright; "that drive revenue" drops into italic. One serif phrase in an otherwise sans-serif world. Feels like a broadsheet or magazine, not a SaaS tool.

## Signature Design Moves

### 1. Persistent floating testimonial widget (bottom-left)
Always visible regardless of scroll position. Cycles through client reviews. A persistent trust anchor that works on every section simultaneously. Star rating + face photo + quote snippet + client name + role.

### 2. Editorial serif italic in headline
One phrase per headline breaks into italic serif. The rest stays roman. "We engineer systems / *that drive revenue*" — no one in outbound does this. Reads like a print publication, not a tech product.

### 3. Light background throughout
Warm off-white `#F6F6F6` instead of dark. Most GTM/outbound agency sites go dark and aggressive. C17 stays measured and credible. The single dark section (forest testimonial) hits harder because of the contrast.

### 4. Real email reply screenshots as proof
Not testimonials. Not logos. Actual reply email images showing real company names (NBA, Feastables, Miro, Shaan Puri). Harder to fake. More convincing.

### 5. Process flowchart visualizations
Each of the 4 process phases has a UI diagram on the right showing actual tech stack connections — LinkedIn → CRM → Email, etc. Makes the "system" claim visible and tangible.

### 6. Single focused CTA — no-cost pilot
The entire site builds to one action: apply for the pilot campaign. No free trial, no pricing page as the goal, no demo-or-nothing. The risk-reversal ("no-cost") does all the conversion work.

### 7. Comparison table (explicit)
A table directly comparing C17 vs. "typical outbound agency" across 6 dimensions. Direct, confident, doesn't hedge.

## Copywriting Tone

- Plain-spoken and direct — "We're not a lead gen agency"
- Long-form and persuasive in key explanatory sections
- Specific numbers throughout: "1 million messages/month," "10 million messages sent," "7000 leads in 5 weeks"
- Addresses objections head-on in FAQ ("Can this work with our existing CRM?")
- No buzzwords — reads like a competent operator talking, not a marketer
- Pain-first framing: names the exact symptoms before offering the solution

---

## What Makes It Distinctive

It doesn't look like an outbound agency. It looks like a sophisticated firm. The editorial serif, the off-white background, the measured purple, the real proof — everything signals credibility over hustle. The floating testimonial widget is one of the most effective always-on trust mechanisms on any service site. The copywriting is long but earns every word.
