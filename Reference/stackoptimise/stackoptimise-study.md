# StackOptimise — Website Reference Study
_Source: https://www.stackoptimise.com/_

---

## Identity & Positioning

- **Name:** StackOptimise
- **Tagline:** "Outbound, engineered." — two words, a period. Maximum confidence.
- **Category:** GTM / outbound agency — positions on tech stack superiority ("$150K tech stack installed in your business")
- **Primary offer:** Free custom outbound action plan — no meeting required
- **Secondary CTA:** Book A Demo
- **Differentiation claim:** 62%+ more positive replies than industry average

| Role | Value |

| Body background | `#201E29` — deep dark purple-charcoal |
| Primary text | `#FFFFFF` — white |

| Purple section bg | `#17023B` / `#230B95` — near-black deep purple |
| Muted text | `#5F5F6D`, `#D6D6D6` |
| Green pulse | `#34C759` — used only on live/active indicator dots |
| Purple flash | `rgba(139, 25, 243, 0.35)` — flash pulse on icons |

| Dark overlay | `rgba(0, 0, 18, 0.92)` |

**Strategy:** Full dark site. Rich purple family — multiple shades of indigo/violet create depth across sections. Blue only on primary CTA. Green used only for live/active status dots. The site never goes light except for comparison table cards.

---

## Typography

| Use | Font |
|-----|------|
| All headings & body | `Switzer` — geometric sans-serif, modern |
| Occasional serif accent | `Times New Roman` — used sparingly for stylistic contrast |
| Fallback | `sans-serif` |

**Key move:** `Switzer` is a clean, contemporary geometric sans. Not Inter, not DM Sans — it has a distinctive personality without being showy. Headlines are large and confident. No editorial serif like C17 — StackOptimise stays fully sans-serif except for subtle accent moments.

## Signature Design Moves

### 1. Two-word hero headline with a period
"Outbound, engineered." — comma, period, done. No subhead needed to carry meaning. Extreme confidence. The supporting line is descriptive ("We're a team of elite GTM Engineers..."), not part of the headline drama.

### 2. Email capture in the hero
Instead of a "Book Demo" button, the hero has an email input field + "Press Send." Lowers friction massively — you don't have to pick a calendar time, just drop your email and the action plan arrives. Smart for lead capture.

### 3. Floating notification-style proof chips
Two identical-style notification popups float beside the "62%+ more replies" headline, each showing "Slack Notification — New positive reply." Mimics a real Slack alert. Feels live, not static.

### 4. Tool constellation diagram
A radial icon map of every tool in the stack — Clay, LinkedIn, Apollo, Smartlead, Crunchbase, etc. — arranged in a constellation around a central C17-style node. Makes the "tech stack" claim tangible and visual rather than a bullet list.

### 5. Real email reply screenshots (not testimonials)
A 4-up grid of actual email reply screenshots from real prospects saying things like "I love your email style!" Unpolished, authentic. Much harder to dismiss than curated testimonials.

### 6. Comparison table with three columns
StackOptimise vs. Traditional Agencies vs. DIY/In-house. Feature rows include: Lead magnet creation, 150K enterprise tech stack, Certified Clay/SmartLead/GetSales experts, 62%+ positive replies, Insights from 5M+ emails, Mobile number generation, Rigorous A/B testing, CRM integration, Run by sales veterans. Green dots = yes, red X = no.

### 7. "We take care of / All you need to do" workflow split
A two-column visual. Left: Campaign Strategy, Infrastructure Set Up, Inbox Warming, Email Optimization, Campaign Management, Lead Handover. Right: Attend Meeting, Close. Simple, powerful framing of the value prop.

### 8. Avatar photos in the navbar CTA
The "Book A Demo" button in the nav contains two small circular face photos of the founders. Adds personality and trust directly into the UI chrome.

### 9. Video testimonials face-cam grid
Multiple 16:9 video cards with founder/client faces and play buttons. Real people, not stock. Creates a wall of social proof that's hard to fake.

## Copywriting Tone

- Short, direct headlines — "Outbound, engineered." / "Reach out to prospects in ways you never thought possible"
- Confident without being aggressive — no hustle-bro energy
- Specific metrics woven throughout: "62%+ more replies," "2m+ emails/month," "1:198 reply rate," "50+ clients"
- FAQ is long-form and honest — addresses pricing hesitation, commitment length (90-day then month-to-month), transparency, ROI timeline
- Positioning: operator credibility ("run by sales veterans, 15 years combined experience")
- Proof-first structure: every bold claim is followed immediately by evidence (screenshots, videos, stats)

---

## What Makes It Distinctive

Full dark site with a rich purple palette — the deepest, most brand-committed color story of any outbound agency site. Every section has a slightly different shade of purple/indigo, creating visual rhythm without chaos. The email capture in the hero is the most friction-reducing lead mechanism in the space. The tool constellation diagram and the workflow split visual do more in one glance than most agencies explain in three paragraphs. The comparison table is blunt and unapologetic. The site earns trust through volume of real proof — screenshots, videos, named testimonials — rather than polished creative.
